# Document OCR Engine Product Context

## Register

brand

## Product Purpose

Document OCR Engine is a configurable document operations engine for internal business teams. It turns messy PDFs, scanned documents, invoices, contracts, forms, and records into reviewed structured data that can be exported or sent into the tools a business already uses.

The landing page must sell an operations outcome, not OCR as a novelty.

## Primary Users

- Operations managers who own document-heavy workflows.
- Finance/admin teams processing invoices and supplier documents.
- Contract and trade operations teams extracting obligations, dates, terms, and risks.
- Technical operators who need API/webhook access after the business workflow is proven.

## Value Proposition

Drop in business documents. Extract the facts. Review the exceptions. Send clean records where work happens.

## Brand Voice

Careful, operational, exacting. The product should feel like an inspection desk, a records room, and an integration console. It should not feel like a playful AI toy.

## Anti-References

- Generic AI SaaS landing pages with purple gradients.
- Giant hero type that crushes the product.
- Repeated icon-card grids.
- Dashboard screenshots that look like decorative filler.
- OCR demos that stop at text extraction instead of showing business action.
- Developer-only workflow tools that alienate non-technical operators.

## Strategic Principles

- Show the workflow, not just the promise.
- Make human review feel like control, not friction.
- Show integrations early because sellability comes from fitting into existing business systems.
- Use visual calm and precision to communicate trust.
- Keep visual calm and precision to communicate trust.

## Proof Workflows

The landing page should make invoice intake and contract intake feel immediately credible:

- Invoice intake proves structured extraction and export.
- Contract intake proves deeper document intelligence and reviewable insights.
- CSV, API, and webhook are the first integration promises.

## Design Risks

- Too large a hero headline makes the page feel like a poster instead of a serious product.
- Too much whitespace without product evidence makes the page feel unfinished.
- Too many cards makes it look like an AI template.
- Too much technical language weakens the non-technical buyer story.
