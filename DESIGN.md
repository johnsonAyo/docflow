# Document OCR Engine Design Context

## Design Register

Brand landing page with product credibility. Design is part of the product impression.

## Physical Scene

An operations lead reviews supplier documents on a large office monitor in a quiet afternoon workspace. The room is bright but not sterile. The product should feel calm, exacting, and dependable.

## Color Strategy

Restrained with one deliberate accent.

- Tinted paper background, not pure white.
- Charcoal text, not pure black.
- Desaturated blue for operational focus and links.
- Soft amber for review warnings.
- Soft green for approved/ready states.
- Avoid purple, neon, and decorative gradients.

## Typography

Use a committed sans-serif system with strong weight contrast. Headline type must be confident but not oversized. The product should feel precise and operational, not editorial or poster-like.

Rules:

- Hero headline should fit comfortably beside the product preview.
- Body copy max width should stay readable.
- Labels should be sparse. Do not put a small uppercase kicker above every section unless it earns its place.
- No em dashes.

## Layout

- Product evidence should be visible in the first viewport on desktop and mobile.
- Give the headline room, but do not let it dominate the entire page.
- Use spacing as hierarchy: tight groups for related content, generous breaks between story beats.
- Avoid identical card grids where possible.
- Use cards only for operational artifacts: workflow builder, extracted record, review queue, integration items.

## Navigation

The nav should feel like a refined product masthead:

- compact but not cramped
- subtle border
- calm surface
- clear brand mark
- primary CTA visible but not aggressive

## Motion

Use subtle transitions only. No bounce, elastic, or decorative animation.

## Landing Page Story

1. Hero: business documents become reviewed records.
2. Problem: document work stalls because facts are trapped in files.
3. Workflow builder: non-technical configuration.
4. Use cases: invoices and contracts.
5. Review queue: evidence, confidence, approval.
6. Integrations: CSV, API, webhook.
7. Security & data control: configurable by deployment, auditable by design.
8. Close: replace manual document handling with a workflow that runs itself.
